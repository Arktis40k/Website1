Shutter Island is a psychological thriller directed by Martin Scorsese. The film follows U.S. Marshal Teddy Daniels, who travels with his partner Chuck Aule to Ashecliffe Hospital, a mental institution for the criminally insane located on a remote island.

Teddy is investigating the mysterious disappearance of a patient, but as he explores the island, he encounters disturbing behavior from the staff and unsettling secrets about the hospital. Strange events, violent storms, and haunting flashbacks begin to blur the line between reality and illusion. As the investigation deepens, Teddy is forced to confront shocking truths about the island, the hospital, and himself. The movie builds toward a dramatic and ambiguous ending that challenges the audience to question identity, memory, and sanity.

Reviewers say 'Shutter Island' is lauded for its suspenseful atmosphere, intricate plot, and standout performances by Leonardo DiCaprio and Ben Kingsley. The film delves into themes of guilt, grief, and reality. However, some find the plot convoluted and the twist predictable. Critics also note slow pacing and excessive length. Despite these issues, many praise Martin Scorsese's direction and the film's striking visual style.

Shutter Island is known for its intense atmosphere, psychological twists, and thought-provoking conclusion. Even with a such a conclusion there are still many fan theories over the movie in which many have scoured each scene of the movie.
